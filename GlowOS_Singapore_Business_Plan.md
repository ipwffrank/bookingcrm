## 1. Executive Summary

**GlowOS** is a SaaS platform purpose-built for Singapore's salons, beauty centres, spas, nail studios, and massage businesses. It combines a smart online booking system with a CRM-powered VIP intelligence engine — enabling wellness business owners to identify their highest-value clients, reduce no-shows, automate retention marketing, and acquire new customers directly through Google Search and Maps.

The platform is inspired by two proven global models:

- **Chope** (acquired by Grab, 2024) — Asia's leading dining reservation platform, which built a consumer-facing discovery and booking marketplace across Singapore and Southeast Asia.
- **SevenRooms** — A hospitality CRM and operations platform serving 15,000+ restaurants globally, focused on guest data ownership, VIP identification, and automated personalised marketing.

GlowOS adapts the best of both for the underserved Singapore wellness vertical — a sector with over 6,000 registered businesses that manage bookings manually, have no structured client intelligence, and are invisible to the millions of Google searches made for beauty services every week.

**Singapore is the ideal launch market because:**
- Reserve with Google is **fully live** in Singapore right now — the native "Book" button appears in Google Search and Maps for wellness businesses
- Singapore has one of the **highest digital payment penetration rates** in Southeast Asia (57% in 2024, with 80% of consumers using PayNow)
- The wellness sector employs ~100,000 people and is valued at over USD 1 billion
- Singapore is a **proven gateway** for regional expansion into Malaysia, Indonesia, Thailand, and beyond

**Core value proposition to salon owners:**
> "We put your 'Book Now' button on Google, bring you new clients you've never had before, and help you identify and keep your most valuable ones — automatically."

## 5. The Google Advantage — Singapore

### What "Reserve with Google" Means in Practice

When a client in Singapore searches "facial near me" or "best nail salon Orchard" on Google, a panel appears on the right side of the screen (desktop) or at the top of results (mobile) showing the salon's name, hours, ratings, photos — and a prominent **"Book"** button.

Clicking that button shows available appointment slots **directly inside Google** and allows the client to confirm a booking without ever leaving the Google interface. This is the Reserve with Google End-to-End integration, and it is live in Singapore today.

### Three Integration Tiers (Progressive Deployment)

#### Tier 1 — Appointment Attribute Link (Deploy: Day 1, no approval needed)
- A booking URL added to the salon's Google Business Profile
- Lowest prominence but zero setup friction
- Every salon is bookable from Google within 30 minutes of onboarding

#### Tier 2 — Place Action Link (Deploy: Day 1, no approval needed)
- A dedicated booking button on the GBP, higher prominence than Tier 1
- Redirects client to GlowOS-hosted booking page
- Salon needs no website — your hosted page at `glowos.sg/salon-name` does the work

#### Tier 3 — Reserve with Google End-to-End (Deploy: After Google partner approval)
- Native "Book" button with live availability shown inside Google Search and Maps
- Client books without leaving Google
- Requires GlowOS to be approved as a Google Actions Center partner
- **This is the target state for all Singapore merchants**
- Conversion rates ~105% higher than appointment link redirects

### The Critical First-Mover Rule

Google's own platform policy states: **for appointment services (non-restaurant), only the first partner who registers a matched merchant with availability data will have their button displayed.** Once GlowOS registers a salon on the Actions Center, no competitor can show their booking button for that merchant.

**This means every salon you onboard and register is permanently protected from competitive displacement on Google.**

### Google Actions Center Technical Requirements (Singapore)

To become an approved Google partner, GlowOS must meet these standards:

| Requirement | Specification |
|---|---|
| Availability API response time | Under 1 second for all requests from Google |
| Calendar depth | Minimum 30 days of availability at all times |
| Booking confirmation | Real-time, automatic — no manual confirmation |
| Online cancellation | Client self-service cancellation must be supported |
| Service pricing | Every service must have a valid SGD price ($0 not permitted) |
| Service descriptions | All services require written descriptions |
| Merchant data accuracy | Salon name, address, phone must match GBP exactly |
| Error rate | Must maintain acceptable technical error rates (Google-defined) |

## 8. Booking Workflow

### End-to-End Flow (Google-Originated Booking)

```
1. Client searches "gel manicure near Tanjong Pagar" on Google
          ↓
2. Partner salon GBP appears with "Book" button (powered by GlowOS)
          ↓
3. Available slots shown (natively in Google or on GlowOS booking page)
          ↓
4. Client selects: Service → Therapist → Date/Time
          ↓
5. Cancellation policy displayed clearly before payment screen
          ↓
6. Client pays via PayNow / GrabPay / Card / Apple Pay / Google Pay
          ↓
7. Stripe/HitPay fires payment webhook to GlowOS server (< 3 seconds)
          ↓
8. GlowOS records: booking_source = "google", amount = SGD 80
          ↓
9. Automatic split:
     → SGD 8 (10%) held in GlowOS account
     → SGD 72 (90%) queued for salon payout
          ↓
10. Salon receives instant WhatsApp: "New booking confirmed — 💚 Paid"
          ↓
11. Client receives confirmation with booking summary
          ↓
12. 24 hours before: automated reminder sent to client
          ↓
13. After appointment: review request sent automatically
          ↓
14. Client profile enriched in CRM: service history, spend, preferences
```

### Booking Source Tagging — Commission Logic

Every booking created in GlowOS is automatically tagged with its originating source. This tag determines whether commission applies.

| Source Tag | Channel | Commission Applied? |
|---|---|---|
| `google_reserve` | Reserve with Google (native) | ✅ 10% |
| `google_gbp_link` | Clicked from GBP Appointment Link | ✅ 10% |
| `direct_widget` | Salon's own website or Instagram bio link | ❌ None |
| `walkin_manual` | Staff manually enters walk-in booking | ❌ None |
| `returning_google` | Previous Google client rebooks directly | ❌ 3% (reduced) |

### Real-Time Payment Confirmation for Salon Staff

Since bank settlement takes 1–3 business days, salons confirm payments through GlowOS — not their bank account. The moment a payment webhook fires:

1. **WhatsApp alert** — instant notification to salon owner/staff device
2. **Dashboard update** — booking status changes to 💚 Paid in real-time
3. **Optional front-desk display** — tablet or TV at reception showing today's confirmed bookings

3. Add it as a Place Action Link (Tier 2) for higher prominence

**Result:** Every salon is bookable from Google within 30 minutes of completing onboarding. No API, no approval, no waiting.

### Phase B — Google Actions Center Partner Approval

**Timeline: Submit application immediately, approval typically 1–3 months**

To become a certified Reserve with Google partner, GlowOS must:

1. Submit the Partner Interest Form at `developers.google.com/actions-center`
2. Build the required API integration (availability feed, booking API, real-time updates)
3. Pass Google's technical validation and quality checks
4. Access the Actions Center Partner Portal to manage all merchant integrations

**What the API integration requires GlowOS to build:**

- **Merchant Feed:** Submit all partner salon data (name, address, category, services with pricing)
- **Availability Feed:** Push real-time slot availability to Google, updated on every booking/cancellation
- **Booking API:** Accept booking creation requests from Google, confirm or reject in real-time
- **Real-Time Updates (RTU):** Notify Google instantly when a slot is booked or freed

### Phase C — Full Reserve with Google (After Approval)

**Timeline: 3–6 months from application**

Once approved:
- All registered salons automatically get the native Reserve button in Google Search and Maps
- Google displays the salon's live availability directly inside the Google interface
- Clients book without leaving Google
- GlowOS receives the booking via API, processes payment, and creates the CRM record

**The lock-in payoff:** Every salon registered under GlowOS's partner credentials is exclusively associated with the GlowOS button on Google. Competitors cannot show a competing button for these merchants.

### Merchant Matching Requirements

For successful Google approval, each salon's data submitted to the Actions Center must match their GBP exactly:
- Business name must match the legal trading name on GBP
- Address including unit number must be identical
- Phone number must match
- Business category must be consistent

GlowOS automates this matching check during onboarding and flags any discrepancies before submission.

## 10. Payment & Transaction Model

### Model: GlowOS as Merchant of Record

GlowOS owns the payment flow entirely for all bookings made through the platform. The client pays GlowOS's payment infrastructure. The platform automatically splits and remits the salon's share.

```
Client pays SGD 120 for a hair treatment
          ↓
Funds received by GlowOS (Stripe Connect / HitPay)
          ↓
Automatic split executed:
  → SGD 12 (10%) held in GlowOS account — commission
  → SGD 108 (90%) queued for salon bank payout
          ↓
Salon receives SGD 108 payout to their Singapore bank account
(Daily / weekly / monthly — configurable per tier)
```

### Why This Model Is Non-Negotiable

- **Guaranteed collection:** Commission is captured at transaction time. No invoicing, no chasing, no disputes about which bookings came from where.
- **Full data ownership:** GlowOS captures transaction values for accurate VIP scoring. Without this, the CRM intelligence is incomplete.
- **Source transparency:** The booking source tag is set at booking creation, making commission calculation auditable. Salon owners see exactly which bookings generated a commission and why.
- **No friction for salons:** Salons do not apply for a merchant account, set up a payment gateway, or handle split payment logic. They simply receive payouts.

### Singapore Payment Methods Supported

Singapore has one of the most advanced digital payment ecosystems in Asia. GlowOS must support all of the following at launch:

| Method | Provider | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **PayNow** | HitPay / Stripe | Instant transfer, near-zero fee, used by 80% of SG consumers |
| **GrabPay** | HitPay | Dominant e-wallet in Singapore |
| **Credit / Debit Card** | Stripe | Visa, Mastercard, Amex |
| **Apple Pay** | Stripe | Mobile-first clients |
| **Google Pay** | Stripe | Mobile-first clients |
| **DBS PayLah!** | HitPay | Popular among local consumers |

### Recommended Payment Stack

**Primary: Stripe Connect**
Stripe is the best choice for the marketplace split-payment architecture that GlowOS requires. Stripe Connect handles automatic payouts to salon bank accounts, supports PayNow and all major Singapore methods, provides full webhook infrastructure for real-time confirmation, and has extensive API documentation for building a compliant Google Actions Center integration.

- Domestic cards and wallets: 3.4% + SGD 0.50 per transaction
- PayNow via Stripe: ~1.3% per transaction
- Stripe Connect payout fees: standard bank transfer rates

**Secondary: HitPay**
HitPay is a Singapore-headquartered, MAS-licensed payment provider with native PayNow Corporate, GrabPay, and DBS PayLah! support. Strong choice as a complementary provider for local payment methods where Stripe fees are higher.

- PayNow/UPI: 0.8% per transaction
- Cards: 2.9% + SGD 0.30 per transaction
- No monthly fees

**Recommended approach:** Use Stripe Connect as the primary infrastructure for split payments and international cards. Offer HitPay as the PayNow/GrabPay payment channel for lower local transaction fees.

### Payout Schedule to Salons

| Plan Tier | Payout Frequency |
|---|---|
| Starter | Weekly (every Monday) |
| Pro | Weekly (every Monday) |
| Business | Daily |
| Chain | Daily + custom settlement |

### Processing Fees on Refunds

Stripe and HitPay do not refund processing fees on reversed transactions. Per the GlowOS Merchant Agreement, the salon absorbs the processing fee on refunded bookings. This is disclosed during merchant onboarding. Typical cost: SGD 0.50–3.00 per refund depending on transaction size.

## 11. Cancellation & Refund Policy

### Default Policy Framework (Configurable per Salon)

| Cancellation Timing | Client Refund |
|---|---|
| 24+ hours before appointment | 100% refund |
| 12–24 hours before appointment | 50% refund |
| Less than 12 hours before appointment | No refund |
| No-show (never cancelled) | No refund |
| **Salon cancels (any reason)** | 100% refund to client; salon absorbs processing fee |

Salons can configure stricter or more lenient windows via the dashboard. The active policy is displayed as a mandatory disclosure at checkout before the client pays.

### Refund Execution

```
Client clicks cancellation link (in confirmation message)
          ↓
Platform checks booking timestamp vs. cancellation policy
          ↓
Full refund eligible  → Stripe/HitPay executes full reversal
Partial refund eligible → Partial amount returned
No refund              → Client notified, no funds moved
          ↓
Client receives WhatsApp: "Refund of SGD X initiated.
                           Allow 5–10 business days to credit."
          ↓
Salon notified: "Booking cancelled — slot re-opened automatically"
          ↓
Commission adjusted:
  Full refund     → Commission reversed
  Partial refund  → Commission pro-rated
  No refund       → Commission retained
```

### Three Refund Authorities

1. **Client self-service** — cancellation link in confirmation message, fully automated
2. **Salon-initiated** — cancel and refund button in salon dashboard (for salon-side cancellations)
3. **GlowOS admin override** — force refund from admin panel for disputes and exceptional cases

### No-Show Recovery

When staff marks a booking as "No-Show" in the dashboard:
- No refund is issued (per agreed policy)
- Salon and GlowOS retain their respective shares
- Churn Prevention Agent automatically queues a re-engagement message to the client 24 hours later:
  *"Hi [Name], we missed you yesterday at [Salon]. Your usual [service] slot is available again — shall we rebook? [link]"*

## 12. AI Agents

GlowOS is powered by seven AI agents, each running as an autonomous background process. Collectively they form the intelligence layer that differentiates GlowOS from any standard booking tool.

### Agent 1 — VIP Identification Agent
**Build Priority: 1**

Continuously scores every client using the RFM (Recency, Frequency, Monetary) framework, enriched with additional signals:

- **Recency** — How recently did they visit?
- **Frequency** — How often do they return?
- **Monetary** — Total and average spend per visit
- **Referrals** — Have they referred other clients?
- **Loyalty signals** — Package purchases, multi-service bookings, therapist loyalty

Auto-assigns clients to VIP tiers (Bronze → Silver → Gold → Platinum) and updates in real-time on every booking or payment event.

Front-desk alert example: *"Priya is arriving at 2pm — Platinum tier, your highest SGD spender this quarter, always prefers Therapist Sarah and usually adds an eyebrow treatment."*

### Agent 2 — Booking Concierge Agent
**Build Priority: 1**

Powers the booking experience when clients land from Google or any booking channel. Handles availability lookup, booking creation, confirmation, and rescheduling. Also enables a conversational booking widget for salons that want a WhatsApp or chat-based booking flow as a supplement to the Google button.

### Agent 3 — Churn Prevention Agent
**Build Priority: 2**

Monitors every client against their personal visit cadence derived from their booking history. When a client goes overdue beyond their typical cycle, the agent drafts a personalised win-back message.

Example trigger: *"Lisa usually books every 3 weeks. It has been 5 weeks since her last visit. Sending re-engagement message: 'Hi Lisa, it's been a while since your last visit at [Salon]! Your go-to [service] is ready for you — shall we find a time this week?'"*

Also fires automatically 24 hours after a recorded no-show.

### Agent 4 — Campaign Composer Agent

Salon owner describes a campaign goal in plain English. The agent:
1. Identifies all eligible clients matching the criteria
2. Drafts personalised messages for each client
3. Schedules delivery at the optimal time
4. Queues for owner review before sending (or auto-sends if enabled)

Example: *"Run a promo for clients who haven't visited in 60 days"* → agent drafts individualised messages for 47 matching clients.

### Agent 5 — Business Insights Agent

Conversational analytics interface. Owner types a question in plain English or Mandarin:
- *"Who are my top 10 clients by spend this month?"*
- *"Which service has the highest no-show rate?"*
- *"How much revenue did Sarah generate last quarter?"*
- *"Which day of the week is slowest?"*

Agent queries the database and returns a clear, plain-language answer with supporting numbers.

### Agent 6 — Review & Reputation Agent

After every completed appointment, sends an automated review request to the client. If the client leaves a rating below 4 stars, the agent immediately alerts the salon owner via WhatsApp for service recovery — before the client has a chance to post publicly on Google Maps. Positive reviews are channelled toward the salon's Google Business Profile to improve their local search ranking.

### Agent 7 — Staff Coaching Agent
**Build Priority: Phase 3**

Analyses staff performance data to surface coaching insights weekly:
- Retention rate per therapist (% of clients who rebook with the same staff)
- Upsell rate (how often staff recommend and convert add-on services)
- Revenue generated per staff member
- VIP client retention per staff

Weekly insight example: *"Therapist Wei Lin's clients rebook at 74% — highest on your team. Her habit of suggesting a follow-up date at checkout is driving this. 4 of her regular clients are overdue this week."*

| **AI Agents** | Anthropic Claude API (claude-sonnet-4-6) | Powers all 7 agents with tool use and structured outputs |
| **Hosting** | AWS ap-southeast-1 (Singapore) | Low latency for Singapore users, data residency in SG |
| **Google Integration** | Google Actions Center API | Reserve with Google End-to-End integration |
| **Analytics** | Mixpanel or PostHog | Product analytics, funnel tracking |
| **Background Jobs** | Bull (Redis-based) | Churn prediction runs, scheduled notifications, report generation |

### Critical: Availability Feed Architecture

The Reserve with Google integration requires GlowOS to respond to availability queries from Google in under 1 second. This demands a purpose-built availability indexing layer:

- Slot availability is pre-computed and cached in Redis on every booking event
- Google queries hit the cache layer, not the primary database
- Real-time updates (RTU) are pushed to Google within seconds of any slot change
- This architecture also powers the live dashboard and handles concurrent bookings without conflicts

### Webhook Processing Pipeline

```
Payment event (Stripe/HitPay)
        ↓
Webhook received by GlowOS server
        ↓ (parallel, < 500ms)
├── Update booking status in PostgreSQL
├── Push real-time update to salon dashboard (WebSocket)
├── Send WhatsApp confirmation (Twilio)
├── Create/update client CRM profile
├── Tag booking source for commission
└── Push RTU to Google Actions Center (if Reserve booking)
```

## 18. Regulatory & Compliance

### Business Registration
- Incorporate as a **Private Limited Company (Pte Ltd)** in Singapore via ACRA
- Register for GST if projected annual turnover exceeds SGD 1 million
- Obtain a business bank account (DBS, OCBC, or UOB recommended for local credibility)

### Payment Services Act (MAS)
- GlowOS processes payments on behalf of third parties, which falls under the MAS **Payment Services Act 2019**
- Likely requires a **Major Payment Institution (MPI) licence** or partnership with a licensed institution (Stripe and HitPay are both MAS-licensed)
- **Recommended approach:** Use Stripe Connect and HitPay as the licensed payment infrastructure. GlowOS operates as a software platform routing transactions through licensed processors, which significantly reduces regulatory complexity
- Consult a Singapore fintech lawyer before launch to confirm licensing requirements

### Personal Data Protection Act (PDPA)
- Client data collected through GlowOS (name, phone, email, service history, spend) is subject to PDPA
- Requirements include: data protection policy, consent at point of data collection, right to access and delete personal data, data breach notification within 3 days
- All data must be hosted on servers in Singapore (AWS ap-southeast-1 complies)
- Appoint a Data Protection Officer (DPO) — can be a co-founder initially

### Google Actions Center Policies
- All merchant data submitted must be accurate and match GBP
- No charges to users outside of what is explicitly agreed at checkout
- All transactions charged in SGD (no currency conversion)
- Online cancellation must be supported
- User marketing preferences (opt-in/opt-out) must be respected and stored

### Consumer Protection (Fair Trading) Act
- Cancellation and refund policies must be clearly displayed before payment
- No misleading claims about services or pricing
- Dispute resolution process must be available to clients
